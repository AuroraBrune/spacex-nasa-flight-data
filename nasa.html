  1. The Founding of NASA
    June 29, 1958

    NASA is established by President Eisenhower, succeeding the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA). The new agency was to have a distinctly civilian orientation, encouraging peaceful applications in space science.

  2. Project Mercury
    May 5, 1961>

    Alan Shepard becomes the first man in space on Freedom 7. It was the first crewed flight of Project Mercury. The project had the ultimate objective of putting an astronaut into orbit around the Earth and return him safely.

  3. First American Orbital Spaceflight
    February 20, 1962

    John Glenn is launched into orbit aboard Friendship 7. Glenn completed three orbits and the spacecraft re-entered the Earth's atmosphere, splashing down in the North Atlantic Ocean. Glenn's total mission flight time was four hours 55 minutes and 23 seconds.

  4. "We choose to go to the Moon"
    September 12, 1962

    John F. Kennedy makes his "Moon Speech" at Rice University, trying to garner support for the Apollo program, the national effort to land a man on the Moon. In his speech, Kennedy characterized space as a new frontier, calling to the pioneer spirit that dominated American folklore.

  5. First US Space Walk
    June 3, 1965

    Aboard Gemini 4, Ed White made the first US space walk. White was one of nine men chosen as the second astronuats in 1962.

  6. Apollo 1 Fire
    January 27, 1967

    Apollo 1 experiences a cockpit fire during testing on the launchpad, killing all three crew members, Command Pilot Virgil I. "Gus" Grissom, Senior Pilot Ed White, and Pilot Roger B. Chaffee.

  7. Man on the Moon
    July 20, 1969

    Neil Armstrong became the first man on the moon aboard Apollo 11, declaring as he stepped down "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

  8. Skylab
    May 14, 1976

    Skylab, the first and only independently built US space station, is launched. The capsule was inhabited for 24 weeks between May 1973 and February 1974. Skylab's orbit eventually decayed and it burned up in the atmosphere on July 11, 1979, over the Indian Ocean.

  9. Columbia's Initial Launch
    April 12, 1981

    The first US space shuttle, Columbia, was Columbia was successfully launched on April 12, 1981, the 20th anniversary of t he first human spaceflight (Vostok 1). After orbiting the Earth 36 tinmes, it returned on April 14, 1981, landing on the dry lakebed runway at Edwards Air Force Base in California.

  10. First Woman in Space
    June 18, 1983

    Sally Ride became the first woman in space after joining NASA in 1978. At the age of 32, Ride was, and remains, the youngest American astronaut to have traveled to space.

  11. "Sometimes, when we reach for the stars, we fall short."
    January 28, 1986

    Space Shuttle Challenger breaks apart 73 seconds into its flight, killing all seven crew members, Michael J. Smith, Dick Scobee, Ronald McNair, Ellison Onizuka, Christa McAuliffe (who would have been the first teacher in space), Gregory Jarvis, and Judith Resnik.

  12. International Space Station Guests
    February 11, 2000

    The International Space Station (ISS), a multi-national collaborative project between five participating space agencies: NASA (United States), Roscosmos (Russia), JAXA (Japan), ESA (Europe), and CSA (Canada), welcomed its first long-term residents, William Shepherd, Yuri Gidzenko, and Sergei K. Krikalev.

  13. "My fellow Americans, this day has brought terrible news, and great sadness to our country."
    February 1, 2003

    Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrated over Texas on re-entry, killing all seven crew members, Rick Husband, William McCool, Michael Anderson, Ilan Ramon, Kalpana Chawla, David Brown and Laurel Clark.

  14. SpaceX Joins NASA
    December 23, 2008

    NASA awards SpaceX its Commercial Resupply Services contract, designed to cargo and supplies to the International Space Station (ISS).animationSpeed The first operational resupply missions were flown by SpaceX in 2012.

  15. The Martian
    August 6, 2012

    Mars Rover Curiosity lands. Rover's goals on Mars include investigating the climate and geology, determining whether the selected field site ever offered environmental conditions favorable for microbial life, including a determination of the role of water; and whether the planet provides a habitat suitable for human exploration.

  16. Girl Power
    October 18, 2019

    The International Space Station had its first all-female spacewalk, with astronauts Jessica Meir and Christina Koch. The spacewalk lasted seven hours and 17 minutes and replaced a faulty Battery Charging Discharging Unit.

The Origins of NASA
NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Act, was created and signed into effect on July 29, 1958 by President Eisenhower in response to the Soviet Union's October 4, 1957 launch of its first satellite, Sputnik I. The 183-pound, basketball-sized satellite orbited the earth in 98 minutes. Its first administrator was Dr. T. Keith Glennan.

Ongoing Investigations
NASA is currently conducting in-depth surveys of Mars and Saturn and studies of the Earth and the Sun.animationSpeed Other active spacecraft missions are Juno for Jupiter; New Horizons for Jupiter, Pluto and beyond; and Dawn for the asteroid belt.
